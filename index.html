About me

Hey! Thanks for making it this far. So, I grew up in Indianapolis and have lived here most of my life. Some would say I have worn too many hats in a short amount of time. I believe it is an advantage because I have more experience and exposure to a variety of situations than many of my peers with the same amount of time in the workforce. When I was done with school, I got into high-volume restaurant management at a place called Kilroy's, if you are familiar. I spent nearly four years there managing a staff of 100 employees. My responsibilities included creating the weekly front-of-house schedule, taking inventory of our liquor, beer and wine supply to place orders with our distributors, as well as responding to customer service issues. I owe my abilty to work, and effectively communicate, with all personality types to this position. Not only did I learn how to lead a staff of individuals by earning their trust and respect, I could become a friend of every guest that walked through our doors. I left Kilroy's because I could not see myself maintaining that lifestyle for a career. Working until 3AM, on holidays and weekends while missing family events is not something I wanted to do for the long-term. I will always appreciate my time there and the opportunity I was given to develop myself professionally and personally. Based on my ability to talk with anyone and everyone, I decided sales would be my next move.
My first experience in the sales world was a high-volume phone sales job in the highly regulated mortgage industry. After passing a strenuous federal exam to get my mortgage loan originator license, NMLS #1647805, I went through an additional 8 weeks of phenomenal sales training. Once I was done with training, I was given 200-300 new leads each day, with a database of 30,000+ old leads, and an expectation of dialing 500 of those leads each day. Lucky for me, I was a high-performer from the start and had a much better rate of keeping people on the phone and going through the sales process with me than many of my colleagues. Within the first 10 minutes of the phone call I could persuade a complete stranger to give me their social security number over the phone so I could get a credit report, and within 45 minutes would have their credit card number to pay for a $500 appraisal fee. My early success lead to my fast promotions first to Team Leader, and then to Training Manager. I really enjoyed training the new employees and helping them find the early success I had. However, I knew that high-pressure phone sales was also not going to be my long-term career.
